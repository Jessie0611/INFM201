Databases are the primary repositories for storing and managing data in most applications. Incorporating databases early in the SDLC ensures that the application has a structured and efficient way to store, retrieve, and manipulate data. Properly designed databases enforce data integrity constraints, such as unique keys, foreign keys, and data validation rules. This ensures that the data stored in the system is accurate and consistent, preventing data corruption and errors.
